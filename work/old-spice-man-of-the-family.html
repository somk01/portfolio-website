Old Spice — Independent campaign concept

Old Spice

The Man Of The Family

A teenage boy, a dadi who remembers everything, and a campaign that ages backwards.

Insight

Inspired by Old Spice's legendary "The Man Your Man Could Smell Like" campaign, this idea imagines how iconic advertising lives on - not through media, but through the people who grew up with it.

↗ Reference: Old Spice's 2010 viral campaign

The film

Opening — a teenage boy scrolling, slightly annoyed.

Teen— Didi, aftershave manga de na skincare ke saath.

Cut to Dadi.

Dadi(calm, confident)— Arre, tumhare dada ka use kar lo. Abhi tak skin dekhi hai unki…

Teen reaction.

Teen(confused, lowkey judging)— Dadi… dada ke zamane ka mere pe kaise chalega yaar.

Dada chuckles in the background. Sister smirks, observing.

Shift in tone — Dadi smiles, nostalgic.

Dadi(softly)— Huhhh — repeat after me.

Dadi initiates the dialogue and Dada joins in the nostalgic moment.

Dadi & Dada— Look at your man, now back to me. Now back at your man, now back to me.

Reaction.

Sister enjoying. Dada laughing. Teen confused but intrigued.

Cut to Sister.

Sister(teasing)— Search kar.

Montage — teen scrolling. Old Spice campaign visuals. Ratings and reviews. Expression shifting to interest.

Cut.

Dada points towards himself — mimicking "The Man Your Man Could Smell Like."

Teen reaction.

Teen(impressed)— Bilkul.

Family together — all smiling. Light claps. Warm vibe.

Sister(showing the phone screen)— Same nikal kr rkha tha. Order kr de.

End card

Old Spice.

The Man Of The Family.

Key takeaway

Heritage brands stay relevant by gently editing the cultural scripts they helped write — and by handing the mic to the people the original ad never spoke to.

Independently developed concept · self-initiated exploration · not commissioned by the brand.
